Origin's(1890-1965)
Squier has its roots in the late 19th century, when Victor Carroll Squier founded a small string manufacturing company in Battle Creek, Michigan. Renowned for the quality of its violin strings, the brand quickly gained a solid reputation among classical musicians . Acquired by Fender in the 1960s, it became a subsidiary specializing in the production of accessories.

The transition to guitars (1980s)
In the early 1980s, Fender faced stiff competition from Japanese copies of its iconic models. To offer more affordable instruments without damaging its premium image, Fender relaunched the Squier name and began producing guitars and basses under this brand. These instruments feature legendary designs (Stratocaster, Telecaster, Jazz Bass, Precision Bass) at affordable prices, while maintaining a recognized standard of quality.

Squier today
Since then, Squier has established itself as an essential entry-level brand, allowing beginners and experienced musicians alike to play on Fender-inspired models. The series has diversified to include Affinity, Classic Vibe, and collaborations with artists.
